Office Admin Quick Guide — FieldPulse

For the office side: creating jobs, dispatching, managing estimates, invoicing, and collecting payment.

A day in the office

This mirrors how the day flows in HCP — just in FieldPulse language. Nothing new to learn, just a new home.

Morning
Open FieldPulse and review the Scheduler

Go to Schedule in the left panel. You will see everything booked for today. Confirm Casey is assigned to each job and times are correct.

Calls Coming In
Create jobs as calls come in

For every new service call, create a Customer (if new) and then a Job. Schedule it to Casey. Job status starts as Scheduled.

Mid-Day
Watch for updates from Casey

Casey moves jobs on his phone: On The Way → On Site → In Progress → Work Completed. You watch the Schedule and Job list. No more calling to check in.

After a Site Visit
Build the estimate if work is needed

Once Casey completes the diagnostic, you build the estimate from the job. Send it to the customer with Option 1 and Option 2. Wait for approval.

Approval
Customer approves → you convert to invoice

When the estimate is approved (customer signs or you mark approved), convert it into an invoice. Send it, take payment, mark paid.

End of Day
Close the loop

Review any jobs still in Work Completed that are not invoiced. Review any open estimates. Flag anything that did not close today.

What is different from HCP FieldPulse separates the job from the estimate from the invoice. In HCP they all blend together. In FieldPulse, a job goes through its statuses. The estimate has its own statuses. The invoice has its own statuses. Cleaner, easier to see where things stand.

How to create a new job

The cleanest way to book a service call in FieldPulse.

Step-by-step

  1. In the left panel click Customers. Search for the customer by name, phone, or address.
  2. If the customer exists, click their record. If not, click + New Customer and fill in name, phone, email, and service address.
  3. From inside the customer record click the Jobs tab, then + New Job.
  4. Fill in the job details: Job Title (example: "Dishwasher not draining"), Description, Appliance Type.
  5. Assign to Casey in the assignee field.
  6. Set the Scheduled Date and Arrival Window (example: 10 AM - 12 PM).
  7. Confirm the status is Scheduled. Save.
  8. Job now shows on the Schedule and on Casey's mobile app.
Pro tip Use a clear, short Job Title. That title shows on Casey's phone and in the customer-facing reminders. Example: "Refrigerator not cooling - follow up" reads better than "Service call".

What the customer sees

  • A scheduled confirmation (if customer notifications are on).
  • An arrival-window reminder before the visit.
  • An "On the way" text when Casey taps OMW.

Trigger for Office
When: Casey finishes the visit (either diagnosed or repair done) and taps Work Completed.

What the office does

Estimates and approvals

The $95 diagnostic, Option 1 / Option 2 logic, and how approvals work in FieldPulse.

The $95 diagnostic flow
Same as HCP — you just build the estimate inside FieldPulse now.

Step-by-step

  1. After Casey marks the job Work Completed, open the job.
  2. Click the Estimate tab inside the job.
  3. Click + New Estimate.
  4. Line 1: $95 Diagnostic. This is always on the estimate first.
  5. Add Option 1 (recommended repair): parts + labor. FieldPulse lets you group them as a single line bundle or separate lines.
  6. Add Option 2 (alternate repair or replacement recommendation).
  7. Note in the terms section: "If customer proceeds with a repair option, the $95 diagnostic is waived."
  8. Save and send to customer via email or text.

Estimate statuses

StatusMeaningOffice Action
Draft You're still building it. Finish and send.
Sent Emailed / texted to customer. Wait. Follow up in 24-48 hours if no response.
Approved Customer signed or accepted (digitally or you marked approved). Convert to Invoice. Schedule the repair visit if parts are needed.
Declined Customer said no to both options. Invoice the $95 diagnostic only. Close the job.
Expired / Follow-Up 7+ days with no response. Follow up by text or call. If no response, invoice the $95 and close.
Important — waiving the $95 When the customer approves Option 1 or 2, update the estimate: remove the $95 diagnostic line (or mark as waived). The invoice should only show the approved repair total. This is the same rule you already follow in HCP.

Your common follow-up scenarios

Customer signed on the spot

Casey tells you or the customer signed via text link. Mark estimate Approved. Convert to invoice. Schedule the repair.

Customer wants to think on it

Leave estimate Sent. Follow up in 24-48 hours. Note the follow-up date in the job's internal notes so it doesn't get lost.

Customer declines

Mark estimate Declined. Invoice only the $95 diagnostic. Send invoice. Collect. Close job.

Customer ghosts

After 7 days with no response, invoice the $95 diagnostic and close. This is the rule — don't let them sit open.

Invoicing and payment

Once work is approved and complete, how to get paid.

Step-by-step — after repair is complete

  1. Open the job. Click the Estimate that is Approved.
  2. Click Convert to Invoice. FieldPulse pulls over the approved lines.
  3. Review the invoice for accuracy — parts, labor, warranty terms.
  4. Confirm the $95 diagnostic is removed / waived if applicable.
  5. Click Send Invoice — customer gets it by email or text with a pay link.
  6. When customer pays online, invoice auto-marks Paid.
  7. If customer pays Casey at the door (card or check), log the payment manually and mark Paid.

Invoice statuses

StatusMeaningOffice Action
Draft Built but not sent. Review and send.
Sent Emailed to customer, waiting for payment. Watch for payment. Follow up at 7 days if not paid.
Paid Payment received and posted. Done — close the job.
Overdue Not paid past due date. Call customer. Resend invoice. Escalate to Morgan if 14+ days overdue.
Payments is already live for you You're already taking card payments through FieldPulse — no new setup. Customers can pay via the invoice link, Casey can run cards at the door from his phone, and everything posts back to the same invoice automatically.
