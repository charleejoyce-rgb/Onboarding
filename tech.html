How we'll build it

Step 1 — Tell us what you need at each step

Charlee and Taylor will ask you: at each status, what are the top 2-3 things you need to do? (Navigate, call, take photo, add note.) Those become the widgets.

Tips, rules, and red flags

The handful of habits that make the day run clean.

The five habits

1. Tap the status as it happens

Not after. The second you're leaving → OMW. The second you park → On Site. The second you start → In Progress. The moment you wrap → Work Completed.

2. Take the photos

Model number, serial plate, the broken part, any damage. Photos prove the story and save arguments later.

3. Write the note before you leave the house

You'll forget details by the next job. 30 seconds of notes on-site saves Taylor 15 minutes of chasing later.

4. If the customer signs off → run the card

You can run payment from your phone. Don't let them say "I'll pay online later" and then disappear.

5. When in doubt — call the office

Better to pause and text Taylor than to guess. Especially on pricing, authorizations, or warranty stuff.

Red flags — don't do these

Don't skip statuses Don't jump from Scheduled straight to Work Completed. Every step matters for the office and the customer.
Don't close a job with no notes If there are no notes or photos, the office can't build the estimate and the customer gets a bad experience.
Don't quote pricing not on the estimate If a customer asks about extra work, write it in your notes. Office builds the quote. Protects you and the shop.

When to message the office

  • Customer isn't home.
  • Job is bigger than expected — you'll run long.
  • Parts aren't in the truck and you need them ordered.
  • Customer wants a refund or credit.
  • Anything weird with the customer, the appliance, or access.
