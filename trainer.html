The insight we didn't have before

The Estimate and Invoice workflows (separate, new as of 3/21/2026)

This is where Option 1 / Option 2 / approval lives. And the diagnostic $95 logic.

Estimate workflow — handling the diagnostic + Option 1/Option 2 12 status buckets available
  1. 1
    Diagnostic Estimate Sent. Office creates a $95 diagnostic estimate and sends to customer on scheduling. Status: Sent.
    Estimate Custom Status: "Diagnostic - Sent"
  2. 2
    Diagnostic Approved. Customer approves the diagnostic (signed on site by Casey). Status: Accepted.
    Estimate Custom Status: "Diagnostic - Accepted"
  3. 3
    Repair Estimate Built (Option 1 + Option 2). Casey builds a repair estimate on site with two line items or two options. Status: Draft → Sent.
    Estimate Custom Status: "Repair - Draft" → "Repair - Sent"
  4. 4
    Option Selected. Customer picks Option 1 or Option 2 and signs. $95 diagnostic waives automatically (configured as a line item credit on the repair estimate). Status: Accepted.
    Estimate Custom Status: "Repair - Accepted"
  5. 5
    Convert to Invoice. Estimate converts to Invoice. This hands off to the Invoice workflow.
    Right-click estimate → Convert to Invoice
Invoice workflow 7 status buckets available
  1. 1
    Draft. Created automatically from accepted estimate. Office or tech reviews.
  2. 2
    Sent. Invoice sent to customer with Pay Now link. Triggered automatically when Job status goes to Completed.
  3. 3
    Paid. Payment received through FieldPulse Payments (already live for you). Status updates automatically when payment clears.
  4. 4
    Overdue. System flags if payment isn't received within terms (set default in Company Settings → Estimates & Invoices).
Key unlock

Because you already have FieldPulse Payments live, the moment Casey taps "Completed" on the mobile app, three things happen automatically: (1) invoice goes from Draft to Sent, (2) customer gets email with Pay Now link, (3) when customer pays, invoice auto-moves to Paid and the money hits your bank. Zero manual office work between "tech done" and "money in".

ClearPath — setting Casey up for success (Week 3-4)

ClearPath is the guided, simplified mobile view for field techs. Only on mobile. Only for Jobs (not estimates/invoices, yet).

What ClearPath actually does

Without ClearPath: Casey sees the full admin interface on his phone. Too many buttons, too many options, easy to miss a step. With ClearPath: he sees only the widgets and action buttons relevant to the status the job is in. Status Instructions tell him exactly what to do. Action Buttons let him do it in one tap.

How it's configured

  1. 1
    Prerequisite: Job Custom Status Workflow must be set up first (that's tab 4).
  2. 2
    Status Action Flow Planning Doc. We (Charlee + Taylor) work through a planning doc together. Four key questions: (a) What are you consistently reminding your team about? (b) What's being forgotten? (c) What prerequisites must be done before moving forward? (d) Best-case/worst-case scenarios on site?
  3. 3
    Charlee opens a Salesforce case. Case Type: Status Action Flow Set Up. Planning Doc attached.
  4. 4
    Tier II builds it in your account. This is a one-time implementation build on our side.
  5. 5
    Casey gets trained on the new mobile view. Typically a 15-min walkthrough.
